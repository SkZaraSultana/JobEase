Interview Rounds

Common Rounds Explained

1. Aptitude Test

Type: Written / Online

Format: Individual participation

About: This round checks your problem-solving, reasoning, math, and English skills. It may include:

  • Math problems – solving quickly and correctly
  • Logical questions – puzzles, patterns, sequences
  • English questions – grammar, reading, and vocabulary

Tips: Practice old question papers and online tests. Manage your time well because questions have a time limit.

2. Technical Interview

Type: Verbal (sometimes written/coding test)

Format: One-on-one interview

About: This round checks your technical knowledge and problem-solving skills. It may include:

  • Explain technical ideas in simple words
  • Write code or solve algorithm problems (for IT jobs)
  • Answer questions related to your field (Mechanical, Civil, ECE, etc.)
  • Talk about your projects or internships

Tips: Be confident with your subjects. Practice explaining things clearly. Give examples from your projects or experience.

3. HR Interview

Type: Verbal

Format: One-on-one interview

About: This round checks your personality, communication, and fit for the company. Questions may include:

  • Introduce yourself and your career goals
  • Talk about your strengths, weaknesses, and achievements
  • Answer situations – how you handle conflicts or teamwork
  • Questions about salary, joining time, or moving to another place

Tips: Be honest, polite, and confident. Prepare a short self-introduction. Show interest and flexibility.

4. Group Discussion (GD)

Type: Verbal

Format: Group activity (6–10 people)

About: This round checks communication, teamwork, and leadership. During discussion:

  • Share your ideas clearly without interrupting others
  • Listen carefully and respond politely
  • Show problem-solving and decision-making
  • Summarize or conclude the discussion if asked

Tips: Stay calm and confident. Make points logically and respectfully. Avoid arguing or dominating the discussion.

5. Managerial Round

Type: Verbal

Format: One-on-one interview (sometimes panel)

About: Conducted by managers or team leaders to see how you solve real work problems. Focus areas:

  • Handling work challenges and conflicts
  • Leadership and decision-making
  • Managing time and stress
  • Understanding teamwork and projects

Tips: Give answers in a clear order (Situation → Task → Action → Result). Show logical thinking, teamwork, and professional behavior.
