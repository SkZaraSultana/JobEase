Interview Preparation

Professional Tips for Interviews

Appearance & Dress Code

  • Do: Wear clean, formal, and comfortable clothes.
  • Do: Keep hair neat and avoid strong perfumes.
  • Do: Maintain good posture and smile naturally.
  • Don’t: Wear casual or flashy clothes.
  • Don’t: Slouch, frown, or appear untidy.
  • Don’t: Use your phone or chew gum during the interview.

Confidence & Communication

  • Do: Speak clearly and confidently.
  • Do: Maintain eye contact and nod to show understanding.
  • Do: Listen carefully before answering.
  • Do: Use polite and positive language.
  • Don’t: Rush answers or speak too softly.
  • Don’t: Interrupt the interviewer or appear distracted.

Research the Company

  • Do: Learn about the company’s work, products, and culture.
  • Do: Check their website, social media, and latest news.
  • Do: Prepare questions about the company to show interest.
  • Don’t: Say you know nothing about the company.
  • Don’t: Give wrong or outdated information.

Resume Preparation

  • Do: Highlight your skills, education, and achievements clearly.
  • Do: Keep it concise, 1–2 pages maximum.
  • Do: Check spelling and grammar carefully.
  • Don’t: Include unnecessary personal information.
  • Don’t: Exaggerate or lie about experience.

General Do’s & Don’ts

  • Do: Arrive on time and greet politely.
  • Do: Stay calm and positive during the interview.
  • Do: Carry extra copies of your resume and documents.
  • Don’t: Complain or speak negatively about previous employers.
  • Don’t: Overlook small details like shoes, nails, or handshake.

Other Useful Tips

  • Do: Prepare answers for common questions like strengths, weaknesses, and career goals.
  • Do: Show willingness to learn and adapt.
  • Do: Be honest if you don’t know an answer, but show eagerness to find out.
  • Don’t: Pretend to know everything.
  • Don’t: Panic if a question seems difficult.
